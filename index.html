FAQ

The questions you're actually about to ask.

What's the license? Can I release a song with one?
Yes. Non-exclusive commercial use - put it on Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music, Bandcamp, merch, music videos, anywhere your music goes. You can use as many covers from a pack as you want for as many releases as you want. Only thing you can't do is resell or redistribute the covers themselves as a pack to someone else.
Will someone else end up with the same cover I picked?
Possibly. These are non-exclusive. With 84-324 covers per pack and a finite buyer pool, the practical odds of a clash are low, but it's not zero. If you want absolute exclusivity, message me and we can talk about pulling a specific cover out of the pack and selling it to you exclusive.
What format are the files? What size?
Every cover is a 3000x3000 sRGB JPG, <5 MB each, named descriptively (e.g. funny_11_vermeer_pearl_airpod.jpg). The packs are flat folders, no nested subdirectories - one ZIP per volume. Each volume also includes an 00_INDEX.html thumbnail grid you can open in a browser to scan everything fast.
What if I don't like any of them?
Gumroad's 30-day refund policy applies. Email seangularity.biz@gmail.com within 30 days and you get your money back, no questions asked. The gallery on this page is a fair sample of what's inside, so you should know before you buy whether the vibe lands.
Why do the covers have no text? I want a finished cover.
Because every release needs a different artist name and title. If the cover came pre-baked with "TRACK ONE / ARTIST NAME" you'd have to edit it out anyway. Text-free at 3000x3000 means you drop it in Canva, type your text, export, done in 60 seconds. There's a 3-step "How to use" above with exact instructions.
Can I get a custom cover or commission?
Not right now. The whole point of the packs is volume + low price. If you want bespoke art, hire an actual illustrator, that's a different product.
